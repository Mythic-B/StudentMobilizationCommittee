The crisis in canadian education

Today, only 49% of the funding of education comes from public funding, as opposed to 70% only 20 years ago. While public education faces budget cuts, banks are registering record profits and military spending hits record levels.

Not Our Fault
Not our debt!

This debt is not ours. Forced Consent, is not Consent!

Government deficits and military commitments decided by generations before us are inherited as our tax burden. In order to service (pay) the debt we owe to domestic & international financial capitalists, we have to bear the brunt of government austerity—funding cuts that translate into rising schooling costs for us.

The Right to Education

Education should not be determine by financial capacity!

The reason why we don’t have free tuition in our country is simple. It is related to the structural crisis of capitalism that forces the ruling class to make the youth, students and the popular strata pay for this crisis, a crisis that we are not responsible for.

Its time to prioritize our future

We have the money to fund education, we just chose not to!

The money is there to invest in public education: it would cost approximately $10 billion to achieve free education in Canada. That is roughly ONLY half of the military budget, which means that if we cut it by 75%, there would be, even after allowing for free tuition for all of Canada’s post-secondary students, 5 billion left for other programs like expanded health care or social housing.
